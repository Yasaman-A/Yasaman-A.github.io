I am a Software Engineer an Assistant Professor at Mount Royal University. I have received my PhD in Software Engineering from University of Calgary on Performance Management of Web Services Using Machine Learning Techniques. My research interests are distributed machine learning, big data analytics, cloud and edge computing.

My journey of academic career started from Amirkabir University of Technology (AUT) in Tehran where I got selected as a top undergraduate student in Computer Information Technology program and was offered to start my MSc education at AUT in 2008. During my research at AUT I had the privilege to work under the supervision of Dr. Mohammad Kazem Akbari in a research team which was following a visionary research plan in the area of emerging computing technologies. My research topic was in the context of pervasive computing which is now known as the Internet of Things.

I started my PhD studies in Calgary where the weather always reminds me of my hometown! My 4 years of PhD studies at the University of Calgary was full of new experiences, learning, and growing. I worked in the area of machine learning and cloud computing under the supervision of Dr. Behrouz Far and Dr. Diwakar Krishanmurhty. Working for a year as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Calgary was an opportunity for me to learn about new areas in computing while enjoying the support of many great people around me during my career transition. In 2018, I started as an assistant professor at Mount Royal University and I am enjoying the combination of teaching and research in my career.

I deeply care about the practical and hands-on aspects of the computing field. To gain hands-on experience in my field, I started working in industry when I was in the 4th year of my undergraduate studies. I started working as a software developer in a team that helped me to connect the learnings from my courses to real-life solutions. After learning more about the software engineering career path, I moved to a software tester role, as my next career. In this role, I worked with a higly motivated and ambitious team buidling automated solutions for testing the functionality of highly sensitive financial services. The creativity and the novelty involved in my day-to-day work motivated me to start my PhD research in the area of software testing. My PhD research, helped me to expanded my knowledge of software testing and quality assurance and focus on the performance aspect of large scale applications. I worked on the performance analysis of cloud-based services and big data applications. My current projects involves building, testing, and modeling distributed machine learning and data analytics applications.
